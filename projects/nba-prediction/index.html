The project

Forecasting the next season—not reconstructing the last one.

CourtVision learns from real season-to-season player transitions, measures its value against a repeat-last-season baseline, and turns the resulting forecasts into a searchable product.

9statistics forecasted
251holdout players evaluated
361qualified 2026–27 forecasts

Leakage-free temporal validation

I rebuilt the modeling pipeline so each training example uses season T to predict the same player in season T+1. The model was evaluated on a time-based 2025–26 holdout that remained unseen during training and improved mean absolute error over a repeat-last-season baseline across all nine targets. The largest gains were rebounds at 9.0%, points at 5.9%, and turnovers at 4.7%.

Sample reliability

Published forecasts require at least 41 games in the previous season. Shorter transitions are excluded from training, while qualified observations are weighted by games played so larger, more reliable samples carry more influence. The final model produced forecasts for 361 returning players for 2026–27.

What I delivered

  • A reproducible Python and scikit-learn pipeline for nine per-game statistics
  • Time-based holdout evaluation with baseline MAE, RMSE, and R² reporting
  • CSV-based, versioned predictions and model-performance artifacts
  • A responsive React and Next.js website with searchable rankings, player comparisons, and backtest reporting
